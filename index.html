Research Interests

3D Molecule Generation

Publications

JCIM 2025 DOI: 10.1021/acs.jcim.5c00665

Structural bias in three-dimensional autoregressive generative machine learning of organic molecules

Z. Koczor-Benda, J. Gilkes, F. Bartucca, A. Al-Fekaiki, R.J. Maurer

Journal of Chemical Information and Modeling — 2025

Three-dimensional deep generative models for molecule generation are increasingly used in property-driven molecular design. This work systematically investigates the structural biases that emerge in generated molecules from G-SchNet, GeoLDM, and EDM — examining elemental composition, functional group distributions, size and weight distributions, saturation levels, and chemical space coverage relative to their training datasets. Significant shared biases identified include the overexpression of heteroatoms across all three models, the overexpression of disconnected molecules by EDM and GeoLDM, and the generation of overly heavy molecules by G-SchNet.

Master’s Thesis · University of Warwick · 2024

Structural Bias in 3D Deep Generative Models for Molecular Generation

University of Warwick — MChem Chemistry
The emergence of 3D deep generative models for novel molecule generation has promising prospects for improving, aiding and accelerating processes of property-driven molecular design. Currently, these models are measured through the validity, uniqueness and novelty of their generated molecules. However, there are many discrepancies with the definition of validity in molecular modelling, along with emerging work showing structural biases in the molecules generated. Because of this, the need to investigate how well these models recreate the training data’s chemical space is increasingly important, if they are to be reliably applied in real-world molecular property-driven design. Here, I investigate three deep generative models, G-SchNet, GeoLDM and EDM, with regard to their ability to replicate the chemical spaces covered by training datasets of both small, simple molecules and larger, complex, and elementally diverse molecules. I investigate elemental composition and distribution, functional group distribution, size and weight distribution, level of saturation, and chemical space distribution for all training datasets and all models’ generated molecules, finding both shared biases across models and biases unique to specific models. The most significant and noticeable biases are the overexpression of heteroatoms in all 3 models, the overexpression of disconnected molecules by EDM and GeoLDM, and the generation of overly heavy molecules with G-SchNet.
